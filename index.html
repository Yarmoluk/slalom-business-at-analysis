Strategic Assessment
Slalom Consulting is a $3B, privately held technology and business consulting firm with elite vendor partnerships and a distinctive local-market delivery model. However, the firm faces compounding structural challenges: its AI offerings are built entirely on vendor platforms with no proprietary IP, the 2023 layoffs shattered its "people over profits" brand identity, and its billable-hours revenue model is increasingly misaligned with an industry shifting toward outcome-based delivery. Slalom occupies a precarious middle — too expensive for mid-market, too small for enterprise transformations, and too vendor-dependent for AI credibility. The firm's trajectory over the next 12–24 months will be determined by whether it can pivot from labor arbitrage to genuine technology differentiation.

Core Strengths

  • Scale: 45 markets, 12 countries, deep local relationships
  • Elite vendor partnerships (AWS POTY, 5x Snowflake POTY)
  • Slalom Build: 16 engineering centers
  • 50%+ revenue from technology services
  • No-travel, local-delivery model

Key Vulnerabilities

  • No proprietary AI platform, model, or data IP
  • ~80% of work perceived as staff augmentation
  • Glassdoor crashed from 4.5 to 3.6
  • Rates ($200–275/hr) undercut 75%+ by offshore
  • 6–18 month engagements vs. weeks demanded
  • Hyperscaler partners becoming competitors

The Local Market Model

  • Own GM per market with P&L and local hiring authority
  • Local staffing — no fly-in delivery
  • Market autonomy — local leaders decide
  • Cross-market staffing as overflow before external hiring
  • Global practices provide frameworks and specialized expertise
Strategic Insight The local model creates stickier relationships than Big 4 rotational staffing. But it also introduces fragmentation: markets compete for talent, delivery quality varies, and there is no unified AI go-to-market. A client in Dallas gets a different Slalom than Seattle.

Slalom Build (2018)

16 global Build Centers for custom software, cloud-native products, platform engineering. Three pillars: Builders (dedicated engineering), Build Centers (in-timezone hubs), Elasticity (scale per phase).

Observation Build is the firm's most defensible asset, but it's undermarketed. Most buyers still think "Slalom = staff aug."

Why This Matters The consulting industry's core assumption — that complex business analysis requires large teams billing thousands of hours — is wrong.
